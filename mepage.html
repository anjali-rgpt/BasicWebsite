Brief Introduction

Hello and howdy to whoever is reading this glorious page. My name is Anjali Ragupathi. I was born in the beautiful month of October in a quaint little town called Kharkov, in Ukraine. My mum is Ukrainian, and my dad is Indian; both are medical professionals, although Mum doesn't really practice medicine these days. I have a little sister, who is entering the annoying stage of teen-hood and is every bit as snarky as I was during that time. We have a dog named Snoopy who is, coincidentally, a beagle. She doesn't fly a plane but she sure zooms around like one. That is our family.

Over the course of my 19 years of existence, I have been shuttled among various locations, including, but not limited to Coimbatore, Pattambi and Ooty. Coimbatore is and always has been what I consider home, mostly because it's so familiar, and it's the place where I've lived the longest. There is no altitude sickness involved. There are plenty of amazing food spots. The weather is pretty decent most of the time, except in summer, when it feels like the devil is visiting with his armada of fiery charcoal pits. It's also pretty safe, most of the time, with a convenient public transport system and a semi-metropolitan lifestyle.

I study Computer Science and Engineering at Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham. The campus is pretty green, and everything is within walking distance. Travelling here takes a good hour each way. The food here...isn't the best. The uniform...not the best either. Oh well.

What I Do

I exist. Mostly.

I spend my days trying to keep up with the dastardly syllabus that we have, whilst still maintaining a composed and sane state of mind. There are projects to be done, assignments to be finished, tests to prepare for. Most days, you don't really have time to do anything else. THe worst part is all the expectations that people place on your head. Quite annoying.

I have a very very tiny circle of friends who are excellent at comprehending the random things I come up with sometimes, and whose brainwaves usually sync with mine. THe easiest way to make new friends, for me is a two-subject thing:

  1. Books, most frequently either Harry Potter, The Shadowhunter Chronicles or some other fantasy situation
  2. Writing

Speaking of writing, I maintain a blog specifically to rant, ramble and recount various scenarios from my life. There will be a link in the contact page. Hope you enjoy it. In any case, writing has always been a significant part of my life, and I've been writing since I was 4, albeit the quality has definitely improved over the years.

Reading is just as important. Currently, I am highly inclined towards fiction of the fantasy kind, in particular, the work of one Brandon Sanderson ( Mistborn,Way of Kings,et.al). I enjoy classics and light reading like The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy, but I do not mind reading any other genre either. At college, I am involved in the book club and plan to organize enticing events to bring together fellow bibliophiles.

I dabble in music, occasionally singing and playing the guitar, but none of this has been discovered by the public eye, thank goodness. I draw on occasion and doodle always, particularly in notebooks where I find I need more mental concentration. An interesting fact is that I am not mathematically inclined in the slightest, by interest, and yet I find myself in a course that relies heavily on this. Anyway. Supplementary hobbies involve travel ( it's amazing when you have funds ) - in particular, food tourism. I enjoy food, although my cooking skills leave much to be desired. Food writing and reviewing is what I'd consider one of my niches to be. Most importantly, sleeping is an art and one of my favourite ways to destress.
